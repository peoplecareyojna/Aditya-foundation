In the bustling streets of India, where life’s challenges are evident, a safe shelter emerges as a beacon of hope for the most vulnerable among us – street and working children. These shelters serve as sanctuaries, providing a haven from the harsh realities they face daily.

A safe shelter offers these children more than just a roof over their heads; it provides a nurturing environment where their physical, emotional, and psychological well-being is prioritised. Here, they find solace in the dangers of the streets, shielding them from exploitation, abuse, and the perils of a life without protection.

Within these sanctuaries, marginalised children are granted access to education, healthcare, and nutrition – basic rights that are often out of reach for those living on the streets. This protective cocoon empowers them with the tools they need to break free from the cycle of poverty and build a brighter future.

Moreover, safe shelters foster a sense of belonging and community among these children. Surrounded by other children of their age group who share similar experiences, they form bonds that provide emotional support and companionship. This supportive network instills a sense of hope, confidence, and self-worth, crucial elements for their personal growth and development.

As these children flourish within the protective embrace of a safe shelter, their potential begins to unfold. With access to education and skills training, they gain the means to chase their dreams and unlock their full potential. They become equipped to navigate the challenges that once seemed insurmountable, propelling them towards a future brimming with promise.

In essence, a safe shelter is more than just a physical structure; it is a sanctuary that breathes life into the aspirations and dreams of marginalised and at-risk children. It offers them a chance to thrive, to rise above adversity, and to become the architects of their own destiny. It is a testament to the transformative power of compassion, protection, and opportunity.

Salaam Baalak Trust, an esteemed NGO in Delhi NCR, provides a safe haven for vulnerable children. Their shelters, including a girls’ residential home in Gurugram, offer protection, education, and a nurturing community. Support their vital work by donating today. Together, we can empower these children to break free from adversity and build a brighter tomorrow.

Street children are those who are forced to live on the streets due to poverty, neglect, or abuse. These children are exposed to a wide range of dangers, such as violence, sexual abuse, and substance abuse. To ensure their safety and well-being, it is essential to provide them with a safe shelter.

The plight of street children is a pressing issue that requires urgent attention. These children are often forced to work in hazardous conditions, beg for food and money, and face the threat of violence and abuse daily. Child labour is still a rampant practice that must be addressed and resolved.

Without a safe shelter, these children are exposed to a variety of dangers, including exploitation, trafficking, and violence. In addition to physical dangers, these children are also at risk of developing mental health issues such as depression and anxiety, which can have long-term effects on their overall development.

Providing street children with a safe shelter is crucial to protecting and supporting them. Safe shelters can provide a temporary home for these children and offer them protection from the dangers of the streets. They can also provide them with access to necessities such as food, clothing, and healthcare, as well as education and vocational training.

Safe shelters can also provide a sense of stability and support for these children, allowing them to develop healthy relationships and social skills. This can help them to transition back into society and lead successful, productive lives.

In the case of young girls, many of them hail from villages and hamlets and wind up on the streets of big cities. Many of these girls have either fled their homes or got separated from them. They are often victims of trafficking, kidnapping, or domestic violence. For girls surviving on the streets alone, each day is filled with numerous difficulties and dangers. They frequently face instances of sexual assault, physical abuse, and efforts to coerce them into prostitution. These girls yearn to be under a roof where they can rest soundly at night.

This is why governments, NGOs, and individuals must work together to ensure that street children are protected, supported, and given the resources, they need to succeed. This includes providing safe shelters, educational facilities, healthcare, and job training, as well as addressing the underlying issues that force children onto the streets.

Salaam Balaak Trust, a charitable trust in Delhi, offers a sensitive and nurturing setting for street and working children and other vulnerable children. It aims to offer children a place where they can realise their full potential and have access to a safe shelter, an education, and good health and nutrition. National Education Day is an occasion that commemorates the need for all children to be educated. Unfortunately, education for street children is still not a reality in most parts of India. The number of children living on the streets and working hard to survive has been steadily rising. Education is not even on their radar amidst the struggle to make ends meet.

Innumerable underprivileged children wind up on the streets for a variety of reasons. Many of them travel to Delhi from their impoverished backgrounds in search of chances and a better life. Some run away from their homes due to abusive and careless guardians, while others have been reported to be cut off from their relatives and are unable to contact them again. The suffering of these children as they struggle on the unpredictable streets of India is a wake-up call for the entire nation.

Presently, children can be seen doing varied random activities on the streets, be it selling knick-knacks at traffic signals, begging, picking up trash, or worse, engaging in illegal activities. This is not the life fit for anyone – definitely not for children.

A Tough Life
The problems that Delhi’s street children face are numerous. Children not only endure a rough life that is frequently dangerous and without shelter, but they also have to deal with violence and brutality. Adults abuse young children, compel them to work, beg, and commit crimes. They are also verbally, physically, and emotionally abused. There have been reports of abuse and violence against more than half of the population of street children, and so much of it still goes unreported. The majority of children lack ID cards or other documents that would allow them to participate in government programmes that supply essentials to minors. The world’s greatest concentration of street children is thought to be in India.

Protection and Rehabilitation
Numerous NGOs have been extending support in many forms to support street children. One such NGO that has dedicated years of effort to the well-being of India’s street children is Salaam Baalak Trust. SBT offers food, clothing, healthcare, education, and most importantly, love and care. Street children are a reality and their existence must be acknowledged as a first step. There are millions of children living on the streets of India, without any adult supervision or support from society. These children are deprived of any of the basic rights – they do not have access to proper education, daily meals, healthcare facilities, or a safe shelter. The children are either runaways, orphans, have cut ties with their family, or are unable to locate them and have no other option but to live on the streets.

All of these children live on their own – they work odd jobs to gather some basic earnings to live off on the streets. Be it in factories or at restaurants, children are employed for odd jobs where their wages are bare minimum and their rights are compromised. Child labour is a major issue in India as children work in unhealthy and unsafe environments. At the age when they should be studying in schools and playing with other children of their age, street children put their lives in danger on a daily basis to earn money. Their mental, physical, and psychological well-being is attacked and deteriorates due to such circumstances.

Children’s mental health also gets affected as children need to be in a healthy environment. They deserve a safe and protected atmosphere to grow properly and healthily. Regular medical tests are also important that must include timely vaccinations. Immunizations can help protect children from avoidable diseases as avoiding these can even affect them for life, or worse, cause death.

Underprivileged children deserve to be around positive, encouraging, and supportive adults. They must be given playtime and tools to grow up to be independent and wholesome individuals.
